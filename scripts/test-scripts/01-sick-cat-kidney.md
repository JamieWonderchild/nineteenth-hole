# Test Script 1: Sick Cat - Suspected Kidney Disease

**Scenario:** Senior cat with polyuria/polydipsia and weight loss

---

*[Start recording]*

"Okay, so we have Whiskers here, a domestic shorthair cat, female spayed, she's about 10 years old. Owner says she weighs around 4.5 kilos.

The owner brought her in today because she's been drinking a lot more water than usual and urinating more frequently. This has been going on for about two weeks now. They've also noticed she's lost some weight recently, maybe half a kilo over the past month.

She's been eating okay, maybe a little less than normal. No vomiting, no diarrhea. She's an indoor only cat, up to date on vaccinations.

Let me get her vitals. Temperature is 38.6 degrees Celsius, that's normal. Heart rate is 180 beats per minute. Respiratory rate is 24 breaths per minute.

On physical exam, she's quiet but alert and responsive. Body condition score is 4 out of 9, so she's a bit thin. Her coat is a little dull and unkempt. Mild muscle wasting noted over the spine.

Palpating the abdomen... kidneys feel slightly small and irregular. No pain on palpation. Bladder is small, she probably just urinated.

Checking her mouth... mild dental tartar present. No oral ulcers. Mucous membranes are pink and moist, CRT under 2 seconds.

Heart and lungs auscultate normally. No murmur detected. Lymph nodes are normal.

Owner mentions she's had no previous major illnesses. She's been a healthy cat up until now."

*[Stop recording]*

---

**Expected differentials:** Chronic kidney disease, diabetes mellitus, hyperthyroidism
