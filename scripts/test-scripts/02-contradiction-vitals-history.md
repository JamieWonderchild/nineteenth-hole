# Test Script 2: Contradiction — Vitals and Medical History Mismatch

**Scenario:** Elderly patient admitted for chest pain. The intake nurse records vitals and history. The attending then takes a more detailed history and finds several facts contradict what was initially documented — including a critical discrepancy in reported chest pain duration and a missed cardiac history.

**What to expect:** Multiple `contradicted` facts across vitals and history groups, plus `new` facts added in recording 2.

---

## Recording 1 — Nurse intake

*[Start recording]*

"Patient is a 67-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with chest pain. Pain started approximately one hour ago. Patient rates pain 6 out of 10, describes it as pressure-like, localised to the centre of the chest.

Patient reports no prior cardiac history. She has a history of type 2 diabetes, managed with metformin 500 milligrams twice daily. She denies any prior hospitalisations. No known drug allergies.

Vitals on arrival: blood pressure 158 over 94, heart rate 102 beats per minute, temperature 36.8 degrees Celsius, respiratory rate 18, oxygen saturation 96 percent on room air. Weight approximately 72 kilograms per patient report.

Patient is alert and oriented. No diaphoresis noted on arrival."

*[Stop recording]*

---

## Recording 2 — Attending history and physical

*[Start recording]*

"Completing the history and physical after reviewing the chart and speaking further with the patient and her daughter.

Several corrections needed from the intake note.

First, pain duration: patient says the chest pressure actually started this morning around 7 am, which is closer to four hours ago, not one hour. She initially downplayed it hoping it would resolve.

Second, cardiac history: patient's daughter confirms the patient had a non-ST elevation myocardial infarction three years ago and had a drug-eluting stent placed in the LAD at the time. Patient says she forgot to mention it. This directly contradicts the no prior cardiac history documented at intake.

Third, current medications: in addition to metformin, patient is also taking aspirin 81 milligrams daily and atorvastatin 40 milligrams nightly — both prescribed after her NSTEMI. These were not captured at intake.

Fourth, corrected weight: bedside scale shows 68 kilograms, not 72.

On exam, patient is diaphoretic — this has developed since arrival. Blood pressure re-checked: 148 over 88. Heart rate now 110. Oxygen saturation has dropped to 93 percent on room air, now placed on 2 litres nasal cannula.

Repeat ECG ordered. Troponin sent. Cardiology consult placed given history of prior NSTEMI and current presentation."

*[Stop recording]*

---

**Contradictions to resolve:**
- Chest pain duration: `approximately 1 hour` → `approximately 4 hours`
- Cardiac history: `no prior cardiac history` → `prior NSTEMI 3 years ago, LAD drug-eluting stent`
- Weight: `72 kilograms` → `68 kilograms`
- Heart rate: `102 bpm` → `110 bpm`
- Oxygen saturation: `96% on room air` → `93% on room air`

**New facts added in recording 2:** aspirin 81mg daily, atorvastatin 40mg nightly, diaphoresis present, on 2L nasal cannula

**Expected reconciliation:** Multiple `contradicted` across vitals and history, `new` facts for missing medications and updated exam findings
