# Test Script 3: Dog Skin Problem - Allergic Dermatitis

**Scenario:** Young dog with chronic itching and skin issues

---

*[Start recording]*

"Alright, we have Bella here, a 3 year old female spayed French Bulldog. She weighs 12 kilograms.

Owner's main concern is that Bella has been scratching constantly for the past few months. It seems to get worse in the spring and summer. She's been licking her paws a lot too, especially at night.

Looking at her history, this started when she was about a year old. They've tried some over the counter antihistamines but they haven't really helped. No fleas noted by the owner, and she's on monthly flea prevention.

She's eating a chicken and rice based kibble, been on it since she was a puppy. No recent diet changes.

Vitals are all normal. Temperature 38.5, heart rate 110, respiratory rate 20.

On dermatological exam, there's erythema in the axillae bilaterally, that's both armpits are red. The interdigital spaces on all four paws are erythematous and there's brown saliva staining on the fur.

Ventral abdomen shows mild erythema as well. The ear canals are mildly erythematous with some brown waxy discharge. No mites visible on otoscopic exam.

There are some areas of alopecia on the flanks, and the skin there is hyperpigmented and thickened, looks lichenified from chronic scratching.

No lesions on the dorsum. No papules or pustules visible. Skin scraping would be a good idea to rule out demodex.

French Bulldogs are predisposed to atopic dermatitis. Given the distribution pattern, the chronicity, and the breed, I'm suspicious for environmental allergies, possibly with a food component."

*[Stop recording]*

---

**Expected differentials:** Atopic dermatitis, food allergy, flea allergy dermatitis
**Expected triage:** Routine
