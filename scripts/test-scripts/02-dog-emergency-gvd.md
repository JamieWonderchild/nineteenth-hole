# Test Script 2: Dog Emergency - Suspected GDV

**Scenario:** Large breed dog with acute abdomen, possible gastric dilatation-volvulus

---

*[Start recording]*

"This is an emergency case. We have Max, a 6 year old male intact Great Dane, weighing approximately 55 kilograms.

Owner called ahead saying the dog has been trying to vomit but nothing's coming up. They noticed his belly looks swollen and he's very restless, can't seem to get comfortable. This started about 2 hours ago, shortly after his evening meal.

The dog is clearly distressed. He's pacing, drooling excessively, and his abdomen is visibly distended.

Getting vitals now. Heart rate is 160 beats per minute, that's tachycardic. Respiratory rate is 40 breaths per minute, he's panting heavily. Temperature is 38.2 degrees Celsius. Mucous membranes are pale, CRT is 3 seconds, so he's showing signs of poor perfusion.

On exam, the abdomen is markedly distended and tympanic on percussion, like a drum. The dog is painful on abdominal palpation, very tense. I can't palpate individual organs.

Attempting to pass a stomach tube... there's resistance, I cannot pass the tube into the stomach.

Heart sounds are difficult to hear clearly due to the respiratory effort. Femoral pulses are weak and rapid.

Owner says Max has never had this happen before. He was fed about an hour before this started. He's a deep-chested breed with a family history unknown.

This is a surgical emergency. We need to stabilize and get radiographs immediately."

*[Stop recording]*

---

**Expected differentials:** Gastric dilatation-volvulus (GDV), gastric dilatation without volvulus
**Expected triage:** Critical/Emergency
