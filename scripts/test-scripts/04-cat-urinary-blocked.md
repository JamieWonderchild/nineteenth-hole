# Test Script 4: Cat Emergency - Urinary Obstruction

**Scenario:** Male cat straining to urinate, possible urethral obstruction

---

*[Start recording]*

"Emergency presentation here. This is Oliver, a 4 year old male neutered domestic shorthair cat, weight is approximately 6 kilograms, he's a bit overweight.

Owner says he's been going in and out of the litter box for the past day, straining but not producing much urine. This morning he started vocalizing when trying to urinate, and he vomited once. He's been hiding and seems painful.

On presentation, the cat is laterally recumbent, dull and depressed. He's hypothermic, temperature is only 36.8 degrees Celsius. Heart rate is 140 beats per minute, which is actually bradycardic for a stressed cat. Respiratory rate is 28.

The bladder is markedly distended and very firm, like a tennis ball. It's extremely painful on palpation, the cat vocalized when I touched it.

Mucous membranes are pale pink, CRT is about 2.5 seconds. The cat is about 5 to 7 percent dehydrated based on skin turgor.

Checking the penis... there's no visible plug at the tip of the urethra, but I can feel grit when I palpate.

This cat needs an ECG immediately to check for hyperkalemia. We need to get a urinary catheter in as soon as possible after stabilization.

Owner says Oliver has had one previous episode of urinary issues about a year ago, was treated with antibiotics and diet change, but they went back to regular food after a few months. He's been eating dry food only."

*[Stop recording]*

---

**Expected differentials:** Urethral obstruction (feline lower urinary tract disease), urolithiasis
**Expected triage:** Critical/Emergency
