### A modern software platform for golf clubs and players

## The Opportunity in One Line

Golf clubs are the last businesses in the world where members have no real digital relationship with the organisation they pay to belong to — and we're fixing that.

---

## The Narrative

Here is the business in one paragraph:

Every golf club has 300–1,000 members. When a club adopts our platform, all of those members arrive on it too — they get a real app, a real experience, their handicap history, competition results, tee time booking. Those members also use the platform independently: they play quick games with friends from other clubs, enter sweepstakes for the Masters and The Open, track their rounds. They bring their friends. Those friends are members of other clubs. They start asking why their club isn't on the platform. New clubs come in through commercial sales and through member demand simultaneously.

That is the flywheel. Club adoption drives member growth. Member engagement drives social pressure on the next club. Each new club makes the network more valuable for every member already on it. The business compounds.

The club SaaS is the revenue. The consumer platform is the moat. Together, they create something a well-funded competitor can't simply copy in year three — because by year three, the network exists.

---

## The Problem

Every golf club in the UK is duct-taping together three or four outdated systems to manage members, run competitions, handle tee time bookings, and communicate with players. The dominant platforms — Intelligent Golf, Club V1, HandicapMaster — are functional but ancient: clunky UIs, no real mobile experience, zero intelligence, and priced in a way that nickel-and-dimes clubs for every module.

On the player side, the experience is worse. Members check results on a noticeboard. Competition entries are done through a portal that hasn't been redesigned since broadband was new. New members arrive and have no idea how to get involved. The club they joined and love has a digital experience that doesn't reflect it at all.

**This is not a niche problem.** There are approximately 1,900 golf clubs in England alone, 2,600 across the UK. Most are running the same creaking stack. None of them have been offered anything meaningfully better. The incumbents have had the market to themselves for fifteen years and have not materially innovated. They are not asleep. They simply can't move — their architecture won't let them.

## The Solution

**Nineteenth Hole** is a modern, beautifully designed platform that handles everything a golf club needs — and everything a golfer wants — in one place.

**For clubs:**
- Member management and directory
- Competition creation and live scoring
- Season series and points leagues
- Tee time booking and utilisation tracking
- Payments (entry fees, member subscriptions) fully integrated
- Bulk communications to members in one click
- Bar and pro shop point-of-sale with daily reporting
- Real-time club dashboard and analytics

**For players:**
- Mobile-first experience across web, iOS, Android
- Live competition leaderboards
- In-app messaging — direct and group
- Tour pools: sweepstakes on majors and tour events
- Quick games with friends (multiple formats, stake tracking)
- Personal scoring history and handicap trend
- World Handicap System integration (via England Golf ISV licence)

**AI where it actually matters:**
- Handicap trend insights and playing handicap suggestions
- Automated competition result summaries
- Member engagement analytics for club administrators
- Smart tee time suggestions based on course patterns and weather

The product exists. It is live. It is being used at our club today.

## Why This Business Works

### The switching case sells itself

Clubs with 400+ members are already paying £4,000–10,000 per year to Intelligentgolf, BRS, or Club Systems. We charge less than £1,500 and deliver more across every module. The commercial conversation is not "will you try something new?" — it is "why are you still paying double for this?" That is a very different sales motion: shorter cycle, lower resistance, no category education required.

### Revenue recovery, not just cost savings

The more compelling case for most clubs isn't the subscription cost — it's the revenue they're currently missing. Clubs running our tee time system routinely find they fill 10–15% more visitor green fee slots once they have utilisation data. At a standard green fee, that's £15,000–20,000 in additional annual revenue for a club of 500 members. Clubs running our bar POS identify 15–20% in previously untracked revenue. The platform doesn't just cost less than the incumbents. It generates new money. That changes the conversation from IT procurement to strategic investment.

### The consumer flywheel

There are over 2 million active golfers in the UK. The majority have no software tools for their game beyond a handicap card. Our B2C tier — already built and live — lets any golfer sign up, track rounds, play quick games, and enter tour pools, with or without a club account.

This matters for two reasons.

First, it's a direct revenue stream: 1% of UK golfers at £3.99/month is £800,000 in ARR from consumer subscriptions alone, entirely independent of the club business.

Second, and more importantly, it's the growth mechanism. Consumer golfers on the platform are members of clubs that aren't yet on the platform. They're playing games with friends from clubs that aren't yet on the platform. They start asking. Club admins notice. Our sales lead's inbound pipeline grows without a single cold call. The consumer product is what makes this a network business rather than a vanilla SaaS.

## Business Model

**Three revenue streams, each reinforcing the others:**

**① Club subscriptions — £100–150/month**

The core. Golf clubs are already paying £4,000–10,000/year to worse tools. The switching case is commercial, not ideological.

**② Tour pool entry fees — per transaction**

The platform runs sweepstakes around the majors and tour events. Players pay an entry fee; the platform takes a margin. No incremental cost. Scales with the consumer base, independently of club sign-ups.

**③ Individual golfer tier — £3.99/month**

Golfers without a club account can access the platform directly. The free tier builds the network. The paid tier monetises it. Infrastructure is built and live.
