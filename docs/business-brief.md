# Nineteenth Hole — Business Brief
### A modern software platform for golf clubs and players
*Draft v1 — April 2026*

---

## The Opportunity in One Line

Golf is a £multi-billion industry running on software that looks like it was built in 2003 — and the people who run clubs and play the game deserve better.

---

## The Problem

Every golf club in the UK is duct-taping together three or four outdated systems to manage their members, run competitions, handle tee time bookings, and communicate with players. The dominant platforms — Intelligent Golf, Club V1, HandicapMaster — are functional but ancient: clunky UIs, no real mobile experience, zero intelligence built in, and priced in a way that nickel-and-dimes clubs for every module.

On the player side, the experience is even worse. Members check results on a notice board. Competition entries are done on paper or through a portal that hasn't been redesigned since broadband was new. Group messaging means sharing personal phone numbers in a WhatsApp group that inevitably gets misused.

**This is not a niche problem.** There are approximately 1,900 golf clubs in England alone, 2,600 across the UK, and tens of thousands globally. Most are running the same creaking software stack. None of them have been offered anything meaningfully better.

---

## The Solution

**Nineteenth Hole** is a modern, beautifully designed platform that handles everything a golf club needs — and everything a golfer wants — in one place.

**For clubs:**
- Member management and directory
- Competition creation and live scoring
- Season series and points leagues
- Tee time booking and slot management
- Payments (entry fees, subscriptions) fully integrated
- Bulk communications to members
- Course card management
- Real-time club dashboard and analytics

**For players:**
- Mobile-first experience (iOS, Android, web)
- Live competition leaderboards
- In-app messaging — DMs and group chats with fellow members
- Tour pools (sweepstakes on majors and tour events)
- Quick games with friends (multiple formats, stake tracking)
- Personal scoring history and statistics
- World Handicap System integration (via England Golf ISV licence)

**AI where it actually matters:**
- Handicap trend insights ("You've dropped 1.4 shots in 6 weeks — time to revise your playing handicap")
- Smart tee time suggestions based on weather and course patterns
- Automated competition result summaries posted to the club feed
- Predictive member engagement analytics for club administrators
- Natural language search across your round history

The product already exists. A working version is live, built on a modern real-time tech stack (Next.js, Convex), and being used at our club today.

The immediate beachhead is the UK club market. At £499/year per club (well below Intelligent Golf's pricing when all modules are included), capturing 500 clubs — roughly 20% of the UK market — produces £250K ARR from subscriptions alone, before player-side revenue, payment processing margins, and premium tiers.

The medium-term opportunity is European and global. Golf has the same infrastructure problems in Ireland, Scandinavia, and mainland Europe. The platform is already multi-currency (GBP, EUR, USD).

---

## Business Model

**Three revenue streams:**

1. **Club SaaS** — Annual subscription per club, all-inclusive. Target £399–£599/year (vs. IG which reaches £1,000+/year when clubs add every module).

2. **Transaction margin** — We process all entry fees and club payments. A 1.5% platform fee on transactions adds meaningful revenue at scale without changing what clubs or members pay.

3. **Player Premium** — Optional paid tier for players: advanced statistics, historical round data, tour pool priority entry, no ads. Target £29–£49/year.
